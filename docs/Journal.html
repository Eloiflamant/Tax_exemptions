Fiscal exemptions for high-skilled foreign workers

Several tax exemptions have been set up in order to attract high-skilled workers. These exemptions are often temporary measures, subject to income levels and eligibility requirements. Specific jobs are sometimes targeted, especially knowledge-based activities (profesors, researchers…).

There has been a clear acceleration of this type of measurement in recent years. Countries that have been rather reluctant to implement them so far (France, for example) have been forced to do so.

Belgium

Exemptions

Eligibility conditions

The special Belgian tax regime is governed by the Circulaire n° Ci.RH.624/325.294 de 1983. It applies to executives of foreign nationality, with positions requiring specific skills and responsibilities. In addition, they must be in one of the following three cases:

  • Being temporarily seconded by foreign companies
  • Being temporarily seconded by a large international group
  • Being recruited by a Belgian company, subsidiary of a foreign company or member of an international group
  • Being a foreign director holding functions in this type of company
  • Being a non-managerial worker but specialized in a field where recruitment in Belgium is difficult.
  • Being a researcher or a scientist

The temporary nature of the activity must be proven, in particular by :

  • the stay of the spouse or children abroad;
  • the provision of a dwelling abroad;
  • the fact that the children continue to be educated abroad;
  • possession of real or personal property abroad;
  • the existence of a life insurance policy outside Canada;
  • a continued participation in a group insurance contract or other savings or pension plan outside Canada;
  • the insertion of a “diplomatic clause” in the lease agreement.

Tax exemptions

Foreign executives residing for tax purposes in Belgium can deduct from their taxable income :

  • expenses reimbursable by the employer and which constitute additional expenses related to the installation in Belgium, including :
    • non-repetitive expenses and charges resulting from the installation in Belgium
    • repetitive expenses and charges: tax equalization, cost of living variance, annual travel expenses etc.
  • remuneration relating to activities carried out abroad: the executive is supposed to be remunerated in the same way in Belgium and abroad. The calculation of the remuneration earned in Belgium is therefore based on the pro rata of the days worked on Belgian territory.

In case of travel, the day of departure is served in Belgium but not the day of return.

Researchers status

A discount of 80% of the professional withholding tax is granted

  • Universities and colleges that pay or grant remuneration to assistant and post-doctoral researchers.
  • Companies that pay remuneration to researchers under agreements with universities
  • Companies that meet the definition of “Young Innovative Company” and that grant remuneration to scientific personnel within this company.

France

The regime of impatriate workers

Exemptions

The impatriate regime provides for income tax exemptions for persons domiciled for tax purposes outside France during the 5 calendar years preceding their employment in a company established in France (intra-group mobility or external recruitment). Exemptions are provided for in Article 155B of the CGI

There are also exemptions for expenses. There are three categories:

  • The operating costs falling under Article 81 of the CGI
  • Specific allowances for impatriates (additional cost of housing and tax equalization)
  • Other specific indemnities (impatriation bonus seen below, personal expenses, home improvement and household appliancesd expenses, automobile purchase expenses).

Other exemptions are possible:

  • The impatriation bonus, which must be explicitly mentioned by the employer and whose amount withdrawn from the total remuneration must result in a reference remuneration (comparable to that of a tax resident in France with the same skills and the same job).

  • The share of the remuneration for activities carried out abroad

  • 50% of income from movable capital from foreign sources (if coming from countries that have a convention with France in the context of the fight against tax fraud).

  • 50% of certain intellectual or industrial property income from foreign sources (under the same conditions as before)

  • 50% of gains on the disposal of securities and employee rights from foreign sources

In addition, the employee can also deduct social security contributions paid abroad :

  • Paid to statutory pension schemes of the Social Security in their home country (8 years)
  • Paid into supplementary pension schemes (up to 5% of the URSSAF ceiling)
  • Paid into professional supplementary pension schemes (8% of annual remuneration up to 8 times the URSSAF ceiling).

Limitations

There is a cap on certain exemptions with a choice :

  • An overall cap at 50% of the total remuneration: impatriation bonus + part of the remuneration for activities carried out abroad
  • A cap on compensation for activities carried out abroad at 20% of total compensation

Duration

The exemptions last until the end of the eighth calendar year since the 2017 Finance Act (fifth year for the previous period). An employee can therefore benefit from them for 9 years if he takes up his duties on January 1st of the year. The plan ends automatically if the employee leaves the company before the end of the term but not if he changes functions.

HQ and logistics center employees

The terms and conditions for the exemption of HQ and logistics center employees are provided for in the Bulletin officiel des impôts.

Both allowances are deductible from income tax :

  • Additional cost of housing in relation to the country of origin
  • Tax equalization

Additional costs must be justified.

Conditions:

  • the employee must not have been domiciled for tax purposes in France during the five calendar years prior to his or her arrival in France;

  • and the headquarters or logistics center must opt to subject these two allowances to a tax calculated at the standard corporate tax rate and related contributions in lieu of income tax on behalf of the employee.

The employee may also benefit from the reimbursement of his professional expenses in the same manner as impatriates, as provided for in Article 81 of the CGI.

Employees and managers of International Chamber of Commerce

Employees and managers called from abroad to take up employment with the International Chamber of Commerce in France after 2011 are not subject to income tax on the salaries and wages paid to them in this capacity for five years, provided they have not been tax domiciled in France for the last five years.

They cannot benefit from article 155B of the French General Tax Code.

Researchers

Some countries benefit from a special agreement with Finland that allows their researchers not to pay income tax on Finnish territory even if their stay lasts more than 6 months and up to two years.

Sweden

Expert tax

The provisions are set out in the Income Tax Act 1999 in Chapter 11, Sections 22 and 23.

Conditions

In order to benefit from expert tax you should either:

  • Be an expert in a discipline where qualified people are lacking
  • Have a salary of more than 94600 SEK in 2020 (111572.16 euros per year)

In addition, you must not have been a resident of Sweden for the previous 5 years. The employment must be temporary (less than 5 years).

Exemptions

If you are eligible to the expert tax, you can remove from your taxable income:

  • 25% of income earned in Sweden
  • travel and installation costs in Sweden

Duration

Until 2021, the measure would run for 3 years. The last amendment extended it to 5 years.
